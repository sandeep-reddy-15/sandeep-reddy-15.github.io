Projects

SeaPULSE: Enhancing Coastal Protection Through Predictive Analytics

The project focused on utilizing Dexwell's SeaPULSE platform to advance coastal protection by monitoring seawater conditions. The Rady School of Business interns conducted comprehensive research on seawalls and employed various advanced techniques to create a predictive framework. This framework leveraged data from seawater sensors, geospatial mapping, and machine learning models, such as the XGBoost Time Series Model, to anticipate and mitigate potential seawall issues. The project also involved developing a detailed market analysis report, business model, and strategic market entry plan, providing crucial insights for seawall maintenance and construction.

Team mates: Vishnu Mohan, Divyamsi Kunapareddy, Xintong Chen
Used Technologies: SeaPULSE Platform, Geospatial Mapping, Machine Learning, XGBoost Time Series Model

Enhancing Market Efficiency through Predictive Ratings: A Deep Dive into the Colombian Business Landscape

This research paper explores the development and evaluation of predictive models for rating businesses in Colombia, focusing on the utilization of various machine learning techniques. The study emphasizes the creation of new features from existing data to improve the predictive accuracy of business ratings. The project initially employs Logistic Regression and Random Forest models but transitions to a custom-built multi-input deep learning model using TensorFlow's Keras API to handle the dataset's complexity, particularly excelling in processing high-dimensional and diverse customer review data.

Team mates: Akhil Chitta, Abhinay Vemulapalli
Used Technologies: TensorFlow's Keras API, Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Deep Learning, TF-IDF

Samsung Mobile: Reducing Churn

Enhancing Campaign Efficiency: Uplift Modelling

In this project, uplift modeling was employed to improve the accuracy and profitability of customer segmentation and marketing campaigns. The project integrated control and treatment datasets, ensuring balanced data through stratified sampling. Logistic Regression and Random Forest algorithms were utilized to achieve a 15% improvement in accuracy. An incremental profit analysis showed a 20% increase in profitability using uplift models compared to propensity models. Additionally, the project formulated optimal customer segmentation strategies, enhancing campaign efficiency by 25%.

Team mates: Akhil Chitta, Ridhima Bansal, Alabhya Dahal
Used Technologies: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Uplift Modeling

Analyzing School Performance: SAT Scores and Mathematics Proficiency

This project focuses on analyzing school performance by utilizing data from a CSV file containing information about schools. The study involves several stages, starting with the calculation of top-performing schools based on average math scores. It then extends to the aggregation and analysis of total SAT scores across different schools and boroughs. The project employs data sorting, filtering, and statistical aggregation techniques to identify schools with high SAT scores and examine borough-level variations in performance.

Used Technologies: Python, Pandas, Data Aggregation, Statistical Analysis

Analyzing Netflix Movie Data: A Focus on 1990s Action Films

This project involves analyzing a dataset containing Netflix movie information, specifically focusing on movies released in the 1990s. The study includes filtering the dataset to isolate movies from 1990 to 1999 and analyzing the distribution of movie durations. Special attention is given to the count of action movies with a duration of less than 90 minutes during this decade. The project utilizes data exploration, filtering, and basic statistical analysis to derive insights from the Netflix dataset.

Used Technologies: Python, Pandas, Data Filtering, Statistical Analysis
