    Gaming Marketing - Uplift Model Concept

    The task involves conducting uplift modeling for customer targeting in a marketing campaign, focusing on maximizing incremental profits. The dataset comprises two groups: a control group (customers who did not receive an ad) and an experimental group (customers who received a randomly chosen ad). The goal is to compare the performance of uplift modeling against propensity modeling in identifying customers for targeting. To address the task, I first prepared the data by adding the "ad" variable to both groups and created a stacked dataset for analysis. Then, using logistic regression, I trained an uplift model and calculated the uplift score. Subsequently, I compared the performance of the uplift model with a propensity model, considering metrics like uplift percentage and incremental uplift. Further, I repeated the process using Random Forest and XGBoost models, tuning hyperparameters for optimal performance. Finally, I determined the optimal percentage of customers to target using both propensity and uplift models and calculated the incremental profits expected from targeting this percentage. The analysis provided insights into the effectiveness of different modeling approaches in maximizing profits through customer targeting.
